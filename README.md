# Project 2: Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA)

## Overview

This project focuses on performing Exploratory Data Analysis (EDA) on an e-commerce transaction dataset. The objective was to understand customer purchasing behavior, identify patterns and trends, detect outliers, and generate meaningful business insights using Python.

---

## Objective

To analyze the dataset and discover useful information through descriptive statistics, visualizations, trend analysis, and outlier detection.

---

## Tools and Libraries Used

- Python
- Pandas
- NumPy
- Matplotlib
- OpenPyXL
- Jupyter Notebook

---

## Dataset Information

- Total Records: 1,200
- Total Features: 15
- Time Period: January 2023 to June 2025

### Dataset Features

- OrderID
- Date
- CustomerID
- Product
- Quantity
- UnitPrice
- ShippingAddress
- PaymentMethod
- OrderStatus
- TrackingNumber
- ItemsInCart
- CouponCode
- ReferralSource
- TotalPrice
- CalculatedTotal

---

## Analysis Performed

### 1. Dataset Overview
- Examined dataset structure
- Verified dimensions and data types

### 2. Descriptive Statistics
- Calculated count, mean, median, minimum, maximum, and standard deviation
- Summarized key numerical variables

### 3. Distribution Analysis
- Analyzed TotalPrice distribution using histograms
- Identified spending patterns among customers

### 4. Product Analysis
- Evaluated product purchase frequency
- Identified most popular products

### 5. Payment Method Analysis
- Compared customer payment preferences
- Analyzed usage frequency of different payment methods

### 6. Order Status Analysis
- Examined order completion, cancellation, return, and shipping patterns

### 7. Referral Source Analysis
- Measured effectiveness of marketing and referral channels

### 8. Monthly Trend Analysis
- Investigated order volume trends across months
- Identified seasonal purchasing patterns

### 9. Outlier Detection
- Applied Interquartile Range (IQR) method
- Identified unusual high-value transactions

---

## Key Findings

- Average transaction value was ₹1,053.97.
- Average order quantity was approximately 3 items.
- Printer was the most purchased product category.
- Online payment was the most frequently used payment method.
- Instagram generated the highest number of customer referrals.
- June recorded the highest order volume.
- Only 8 outliers were detected using the IQR method.
- Customer demand remained relatively balanced across all product categories.
- Most transactions were concentrated in lower and medium spending ranges.
- A small number of high-value transactions increased the overall average order value.

---

## Business Insights

- Digital marketing channels, especially Instagram, contributed significantly to customer acquisition.
- Multiple payment methods were actively used, indicating the importance of offering diverse payment options.
- Product demand was evenly distributed, reducing dependence on a single product category.
- Higher sales activity during the first half of the year suggests possible seasonal trends.
- High-value transactions should be monitored separately for premium customer analysis.

---

## Conclusion

The Exploratory Data Analysis successfully revealed important customer behavior patterns, product performance trends, payment preferences, referral effectiveness, and seasonal sales trends. The insights generated from this analysis can support data-driven decision making, business reporting, dashboard development, and future predictive analytics projects.
